Why I Switched from VS Code to Neovim After Ten Years

It started, as these things often do, with a slow afternoon and a YouTube rabbit hole. Someone had posted a screencast of their Neovim setup—fluid, minimal, almost musical in the way keystrokes flowed into actions. I watched it twice. Then I closed VS Code for the last time.

After a decade of living inside Microsoft's editor, the switch wasn't about productivity metrics or startup times. It was about something harder to quantify: the feeling of working with my tools instead of around them. VS Code had become comfortable in the way a well-worn couch is comfortable—you stop noticing that it doesn't quite support your back anymore.

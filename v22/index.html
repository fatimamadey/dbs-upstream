Cover Story

The Year Cloud Computing Changed Everything — A Retrospective

It began with a whisper and ended with a roar. In 2025, cloud computing crossed a threshold that few had predicted: it became invisible. Not in the sense that it disappeared, but in the way electricity did a century ago — so woven into daily life that its absence, not its presence, became the story. From the migration of entire national healthcare systems to the cloud-native startups that unseated Fortune 500 incumbents, this is the definitive account of the year everything moved upward.

The Woman Rebuilding DARPA's AI Division

Dr. Priya Narayanan doesn't look like someone who terrifies defense contractors. Soft-spoken, with reading glasses perpetually perched on her forehead, she took the helm of DARPA's AI Next initiative eighteen months ago. Since then, she has restructured the entire division, killed three legacy programs, and greenlit a controversial push toward open-source military AI. Her critics call it reckless. Her supporters call it overdue. We spent a week in her orbit.

How Tokyo Became the World's Smartest City

Walk through Shibuya today and you'll notice something strange: the crowds move faster, but the streets feel calmer. That paradox sits at the heart of Tokyo's decade-long smart city transformation. With 14,000 IoT sensors per square kilometer, AI-optimized transit that reduced commute times by 23%, and an aging population that now relies on robotic home care, the city has become a living blueprint for urban intelligence — and a cautionary tale about surveillance.

The Ethics of Predictive Policing in the Age of AI

In Memphis, an algorithm flags a neighborhood as "high risk" every Monday morning. In Amsterdam, a similar system was quietly dismantled after public outcry. Our six-month investigation across twelve cities reveals a patchwork of policies, a vacuum of oversight, and communities caught in the crosshairs of technology they never consented to. The data is damning — and the debate is far from settled.

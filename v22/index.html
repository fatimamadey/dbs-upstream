What's Next for AI in 2026

AI companies will race to build agents that can take actions in the real world, while regulators struggle to keep pace. The EU AI Act's most substantive obligations take effect in August 2026, reshaping how high-risk systems are built and deployed. From autonomous coding assistants to agentic workflows that book flights and manage portfolios, the coming year will test whether the technology can deliver on its boldest promises — and whether governance frameworks can evolve fast enough to matter.

Starcloud Raises $170M to Build an 88,000-Satellite Orbital Compute Constellation

The orbital compute startup raised $170 million at a $1.1 billion valuation, betting that the AI boom will push data center economics beyond Earth with near-continuous solar power in orbit. If the company delivers on its roadmap, the first operational satellites could be processing workloads by late 2027 — redefining what it means to run computation "in the cloud."

UK Bets Big on Homegrown Fusion and Quantum

The UK is investing £2 billion in quantum computing and £2.5 billion in nuclear fusion in a bid to secure technological independence and nurture homegrown scientific talent. The move represents one of the largest single commitments to fundamental science in British history — and a direct challenge to American and Chinese dominance in both fields.

ChatGPT Users Switch to Claude After OpenAI Pentagon Deal

OpenAI's agreement to deploy AI on Department of Defense classified networks triggered the #QuitGPT movement with 2.5 million supporters. Anthropic refused the same deal on ethical grounds, sending Claude to #1 on the App Store. The exodus marks one of the largest consumer shifts in AI history — and raises uncomfortable questions about the relationship between Silicon Valley and the Pentagon.

DOE Quantum Research Centers Reach Breakthrough Toward Scalable Quantum Computers

Researchers at Fermilab and MIT Lincoln Laboratory trapped and manipulated ions using in-vacuum cryoelectronics, a key step toward building large-scale ion-trap quantum computing systems. The breakthrough represents years of painstaking work at the intersection of particle physics and quantum information science — and could accelerate the timeline for practical quantum computers by a decade.
